Your Electric BMW is Finally Here

Jon Wilde
July 29, 2013 10:56 am
Facebook
Twitter
Email

Meet the new BMW i3—for, like, the third time. BMW first showed a concept version two years ago, and we covered the second concept version in our April issue. But this i3, it’s the real deal. And it’s pretty damned cool.

For one reason, the battery-powered i3 does not look like a frog fired from a slingshot (cough, Nissan Leaf) or cost six almost figures (’sup, Tesla?). It’s priced at just over $41,000—before whatever green subsidies you can wrangle. That gets you 80-to-100 miles on a full charge and the sense of success that comes with rolling in a Bimmer.

A carbon-fiber reinforced plastic frame protects your fragile meat sack of a body in a crash, but all the i3’s body panels are dent-resistant plastic. "It’s basically a bumper all the way around," said BMW’s design head Adrian van Hooydonk at the launch event this morning. Makes sense, since BMW is pushing the i3 as the perfect city car, and every vehicle parked on my Brooklyn block looks like it spent time as a target at a driving range.

If you like what you hear, you’ll have to wait a bit longer—the BMW i3 goes on sale next spring. Now, BMW, we’re just waiting on the i3’s all-electric big brother, the sexy i8 supercar. Please?

The interior is just as eco-geek as the rest of i3. Its tweed-look fabric is made from recycled plastic bottles, and the trim on the flowing dash is pressed grass. Even the freaking key—constructed of a plastic made from the oil of castor seeds— would make Captain Planet happy.

A nifty, tech-y detail borrowed from cell phones: the i3’s LED taillights shine through the plastic tailgate.

Coach-style doors—the rear ones open in reverse—and a completely flat floor make it easy to load the i3 with kids or slightly drunk friends.
